Ryan Trine

Chief Executive Officer
CASP+Security+Network+CCNA

Ryan is an Army veteran and senior cybersecurity governance and risk management professional with nearly 15 years of experience leading enterprise risk assessments, compliance programs, and third-party security initiatives across complex and regulated environments.

His background includes extensive work in cybersecurity governance, particularly in governance, risk, and compliance (GRC), vendor and third-party risk management, and audit readiness aligned with frameworks such as NIST 800-53 and ISO 27001. Ryan has advised senior leaders on cybersecurity posture, risk prioritization, and control effectiveness across cyber, physical, and personnel security domains.

Known for his ability to communicate cybersecurity risk in clear business terms, Ryan enables executives and boards to make informed, defensible decisions. He has led independent security assessments, authored executive-level reports, and developed policies and SOPs that align cybersecurity controls with organizational objectives, while incorporating digital forensics where necessary.

Ryan holds a Bachelor in Security Management with a concentration in Cybersecurity, a Masters in Homeland Security with a focus in Cybersecurity, and a Masters in Management with an emphasis in Organizational Leadership. He also holds a Graduate Certificate in Organizational Crisis Management and maintains multiple industry certifications, including CASP+, Security+, Network+, and CCNA.

Luis Nieves

Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
CISSPCCSP

Luis Nieves is an Army veteran and senior cybersecurity executive with over 21 years of experience advising leadership teams on enterprise risk, resilience, and cybersecurity strategy across healthcare, federal, and regulated industries. He specializes in aligning security programs with business objectives, enabling organizations to operate securely while meeting regulatory, legal, and cyber insurance expectations.

Throughout his career, Luis has led large-scale cybersecurity initiatives supporting complex organizations, with a focus on strengthening governance, improving risk visibility, and enhancing organizational readiness against evolving threats such as ransomware, fraud, and insider risk. His leadership has contributed to the protection of critical operations at scale, while driving measurable improvements in security posture and incident preparedness.

Luis is recognized for his ability to communicate cybersecurity risk in clear business terms, equipping boards, executives, and stakeholders with the insight needed to make informed decisions. He routinely works alongside legal counsel, compliance leaders, and insurance partners to ensure that security strategies are defensible, auditable, and aligned with broader enterprise risk management frameworks.

His approach centers on building sustainable security programs that balance protection, operational efficiency, and growth. This includes establishing governance structures, defining risk tolerance, and implementing scalable processes that support long-term resilience rather than short-term technical fixes.

Luis holds a Bachelor's degree in Information Security Management and a Master of Science in Cybersecurity, along with industry-recognized certifications including CISSP and CCSP. He also serves as an Adjunct Professor of Cybersecurity, contributing to leadership development and workforce readiness in the field.
